# Conveyor Maintenance Guide

## Routine Inspection

Technicians should inspect belt condition, belt tension, pulley alignment,
bearing housing, lubrication condition, motor mounting bolts, and the cooling
path around the motor.

## Bearing Inspection Procedure

When vibration and temperature rise together, inspect the bearing housing first.
Check for abnormal noise, heat near the housing, lubrication breakdown, damaged
seals, and mechanical play. Reduce load before inspection if the machine is in a
warning or critical state.

## Belt Misalignment Procedure

Belt misalignment is suspected when vibration increases while throughput or belt
speed drops. Inspect belt tracking, pulley alignment, belt tension, edge wear,
and foreign objects near the conveyor path.

## Overload Procedure

Motor overload is suspected when current rises strongly together with load,
temperature, or RPM instability. Inspect for blocked rollers, excessive payload,
drive configuration issues, and mechanical resistance.

## Maintenance Priority

- Critical: Stop or reduce operation and inspect immediately.
- High: Inspect within 24 hours.
- Medium: Inspect within 48 hours.
- Watch: Continue monitoring and schedule routine inspection.
