# Bearing Failure Notes

## Typical Bearing Wear Pattern

Bearing wear often appears as a gradual increase in vibration followed by a
temperature rise near the bearing housing. Current can also increase slightly
because mechanical resistance rises as bearing condition degrades.

## Evidence Pattern

A likely bearing-wear incident may include:

- Vibration increasing beyond the warning range.
- Temperature rising at the same time as vibration.
- Current increasing moderately.
- RPM becoming less stable during the affected period.

## Recommended Action

Inspect the bearing housing, lubrication condition, seals, and mechanical play.
If vibration is high or temperature is rising quickly, reduce load before
continuing production.

## Uncertainty

Sensor evidence can suggest bearing wear, but physical inspection is required to
confirm actual bearing damage.
