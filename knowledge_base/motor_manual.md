# Conveyor Motor Manual

## Equipment Overview

The conveyor motor assembly on Factory Line 1 drives a belt through a motor,
gearbox, bearing housing, pulley, and belt-tension system. The motor is expected
to operate continuously during production mode when load and temperature remain
within the defined operating range.

## Normal Operating Limits

The recommended operating range for the motor is:

- Temperature: 40 °C to 75 °C during normal production.
- Vibration: 0.1 mm/s to 0.8 mm/s during stable operation.
- Motor current: 5 A to 12 A during normal loading.
- RPM: 1200 to 1800 depending on load and belt-speed target.

Short excursions can occur during load transitions, but sustained values above
the normal operating range should be investigated.

## High Temperature Guidance

If motor temperature rises above 82 °C, the system should be monitored closely.
If temperature approaches or exceeds 90 °C, reduce load and inspect the cooling
path, motor surface temperature, and ventilation around the motor housing.

## High Vibration Guidance

Sustained vibration above 1.4 mm/s indicates a warning condition. Vibration near
or above 2.5 mm/s can indicate bearing degradation, pulley imbalance, loose
fasteners, or belt misalignment.

## Current Draw Guidance

Motor current above 14 A suggests elevated load or mechanical resistance.
Current above 15 A should be treated as a high-risk condition if accompanied by
temperature rise or RPM instability.
